Welcome to Weather Anomalies!

We are going to teach you more about climate. Climate change is inevitable. However, climate is not "felt" like weather is. As a result, amount of action taken around climate change is low. Our visualization shows you the weather anomalies from years 1960 - 2016. Take a look and you'll see the overall changes.

WHAT IS A BASELINE?
We use a temperature baseline to show the changes in temperature over decades-scale time. What we refer to as a baseline is a static temperature - a single value to which all temperature values will be compared. Each county gets its own baseline for each month, which we define as the average temperature of the county from 1960 to 1970.

WHAT IS AN ANOMALY?
All our temperatures are displayed as anomalies. NOAA defines an anomaly as any deviation from the baseline, either positive of negative. This allows us to view temperature differences as minute at 1 degrees, versus trying to compare 50C to 51C between month-to-month.
